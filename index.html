01 · about

From torque to type-safety.

A short version of how a mechanical engineering degree turned into a full-stack development career.

I started in Mechanical Engineering at VIT Bhopal, and along the way got pulled toward building software instead of building hardware. Rather than a side hobby, I treated the shift seriously — enrolling in CDAC Hyderabad's PG-DAC program straight after graduating, to get structured, intensive training in Java, .NET, data structures, databases, and full-stack development.

That foundation now shows up in how I build: REST APIs with Spring Boot and Spring Security, JWT-based auth, MySQL-backed persistence, and React front ends wired together with Axios and React Router. I like projects with a real user on the other end — AgroConnect exists because farmers needed a place to actually talk to each other and to experts, not because it was a tutorial topic.

Off-keyboard, I play competitive cricket — won a district-level tournament — and I'm fairly serious about music, both listening and singing. Both are useful resets for the kind of focus debugging a JWT filter chain demands.
